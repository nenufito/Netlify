TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS

The Ferrari F40 is powered by a 2.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8, developed with direct inspiration from motorsport. It produces 478 horsepower, an astonishing figure for the late 1980s. Paired with a 5-speed manual transmission and rear-wheel drive, it accelerates from 0 to 100 km/h in around 4 seconds and reaches a top speed of over 320 km/h. With no electronic driving aids, the F40 delivers a raw, mechanical, and uncompromising driving experience.

Its lightweight construction, aggressive aerodynamics, and precise mechanical setup deliver an intense and engaging driving experience. Every input is immediate and unfiltered, demanding skill and respect from the driver while rewarding them with a level of connection rarely found in modern supercars.

ACABADOS

The Ferrari F40’s finishes reflect a radical philosophy: performance above all else. Its bodywork is constructed from carbon fiber, Kevlar, and aluminum, designed purely for lightness and aerodynamic efficiency. The interior is intentionally minimalistic, featuring bucket seats, exposed surfaces, and an absence of unnecessary luxury. Every detail serves a purpose, reinforcing the F40’s identity as a road-legal race car and a true automotive icon.
